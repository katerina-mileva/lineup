COLOUR CHOICES:

    • #000000 The black color is heavily associated with rock music in general. The genre is know for connected to a more dark pallete , but also the black color is a classic one that always helps in achieving good contrast which is always a goal within design to make sure content is easily readable.
    • #330C3D Similarly to the black purple is known to be associated with hard rock music especially and it is also used to provide a more original appearance of the website so we have two variations of darker colors
    • #DF3838 The Red is widely used especially in logos of hard rock so it benefits into representing the hard rock music aesthetic throughout the website. Also it has been utilized as an accent color throughout the website to make the design lively, but still in line with the rock aesthetic. It represents the freedom of the members of the hard rock community and their prominent fiery temperament. 
    • #C09FC9 Lilac in our pallete provides lighter accents through the website. It is a variation of the dark purple in the pallete and it perefectly fits into the color story of the website. It represents the softer side of the hard rock community connected to the feeling of acceptance its members feel when they enjoy their favourite music together.
    • #F9F8F1 The white represents the freedom that the hard rock genre pprovides as it has been described during our research interviews. Freedom is crucial to our brand since it is also one of our values.

For the headings fonts we used "Aclonica" font from Google Fonts. Being a sans-serif font it fits a stronger asthetic of the hard rock music and it is also very good in terms of readability. However, its special endings of letters especially obvious in the letter "n" resemble hard rock logos of popular groups. Overall it provides a balance between being strong, useful, and in line with the hard rock music aesthetic. Since we used the font sizes from Bootstrap we decided for the sake of good hierarchy to use "H4" instead of "H3".

For the Body Text of the website we utilized a qidely popular font in web design again provided by Google Fonts, namely "Nunito" in its "Regular" variation. It's a rule of thumb to use a sans-serif font for the body text in design inteded for screens due to the importance of readability. You can see it demonstrated throughout in this current text.

  • THE STRUCTURE OF THE NAVIGATION AND CONTENT (E.G. HOW CONTENT UNITS ARE DISTRIBUTED ACROSS PAGES)

    • Since or website has three main functionalities- information about concerts, information about venues and a community page similar to a Reddit we decided to split the website between 4 pages including the Home/Landing Page. As with any other website information architecture is important to ensure that the users can easily find everything they need in the website.

    The Home/Landing page has several sections

    1. Hero Section that has a cathcy heading and follows the "F"-rule that is connected to the way that individuals process information namely in what direction. Also it has a "Call to Action" button, which encourages them to sign up to our website
    2. A section with upcoming concerts that leads to the Concert page through a button "See More". The section can be seen when you open the website just a bit to draw the user's attention. This is a commonly used trick in UI to encourage users to scroll down on the page.
    3. A section promoting the benefits of the Venues page and what information is available there. It also features icons of thw functions so that the user can quickly skim and understand the information even withou reding.Again we also have a "See More" that leads to the Venues Page
    4. Our last two sections showcase our brands's vision and values. They inform the customers about what we believe in as a brand and provides a bit of background on our motivations for creating it.

    Testing Report

    • YOUR TESTING GOALS IN OTHER WORDS, WHAT YOU ARE TRYING TO LEARN ABOUT YOUR WEBSITE?

      • During the testing session we had several goals. First of all we wanted to test its basic functions namely if everything works properly as it should be or is something improperly showing up or not connected correctly. Secondly, we wanted to test how does the information architecture work and is it intuitive enough for the participants to be able to complete their tasks. Thirdly, we wanted to see if the information provided is not only properly organized but also enough. That is important for us since our website is not only a place for fans to connect but to also receive information about concerts and venues. Third of all, we wanted to see if our brand's values and vision resonate with our target audience. And last, but not least, we were curious to understand if the overall aesthetic of the website matches the vibe of the hard rock community. Also we want one of our testing sessions to be on the mobile version of the website to make sure it is responsive and works well on at least two different types of devices (desktop and mobile).

    • YOUR TESTING METHODS, WHICH INCLUDES INFORMATION ABOUT:
      • The test's participants (number, match with target audience, etc.)

        • We want to test the website with five participants. All of them should fit our target audience of individuals between the ages of 18 to 24 years, living in either the Netherlands, the UK, Belgium, Germany. They should also be hard rock fans who go to hard rock concerts. We achieved that since from our participants two were aged 20, one 21, one 24 and one 23. Their current place of residence is the Netherlands and they all listen to hard rock music and go to concerts.

      • The test’s setting (which material is used, is it done remotely, on campus, at home, etc.)

        • Three out of the tests were done in person and the remaining two online. The tasks and the questions for the testing session will be given by the testing moderator.

        The location of the test sessions varies, but was not deemed relevant so we have a combination of on-campus and home interviews, which depended on the schedule of all parties involved.

      • The test’s protocol (what instructions are given, how it is recorded, etc.)

        • At the beginning of the test session we requested that the participants introduced themselves to confirm that they are indeed with the aforementioned target audience. We asked about general demographic information, but also about what music they listen to and how often they go to concerts. After that we gave the following short summary of the purpose of the website for some context: We have noticed that hard rock fans in your age group struggle with finding people from their friends that want to go to hard rock concerts. They also want to have a community and a place that has info about concerts and the venues at the same place. This is basically how we have constructed the website.

        After that we gave them instructions about the reglament of the testing session: Also now I’m going to give you some tasks to complete that will explore the usability of the website. What I would also like to ask you is to “think aloud” so basically vocalize all of your thoughts during the process so that we gain deep insight into how you react to the website. There will also be other questions during the testing session, but remember that there are no wrong answers and we are interested in your honest opinion.

        After that the following tasks were given:

        1. Your first task is to tell me where and when is the next concert of Guns N Roses.
        2. OK, so if we look at this page about Guns N Roses what will you press if you wanted to indicate that you will be at the concert?
        3. In terms of this page what do you think of its value?
        4. Where would you press in this page if you wanted to connect with other people who are also going to the same concert as you?
        5. Now that we are at the Community page can you describe how do you interpret its purpose?
        6. Now could you please show me where would you go if you wanted to find more information about Johan Cruyff Arena?
        7. When looking at what is available on this page of the venue, what information you see missing?
        8. How would you describe the available information on the page in terms of usefulness?
        9. Now if we go back to the home page, I would like to ask you to take a look at it first and read through our vision and values?
        10. After you have read the content how well do you think those resonate with you as a part of the hard rock community?

        After the tasks some follow-up questions were asked

        1. First of all, in terms of functionality of the website how would you describe your experience?
        2. First of all, in terms of functionality of the website how would you describe your experience?
        3. How do you envision this platform being a part your life?
        4. From the features that we explored can you rank them by the probability of you using them?
        5. If you could describe LineUp with a few words how would you?

        Recording the testing sessions was important since that would enable us to analyze the results in detail. For the two desktop in-person testing session we used a software call "OBS Studio" to record the screen and the audio of the testing session and for the mobile in-person one we used the "Screen Record" function that Apple iPhones offer. For the online testing sessions we used Microsoft Teams and invited a participant to a meeting that was recorded, while they were sharing their screens so again we had both audio and video of the sessions. That was completed successfully and the only technical issue that we encountered was that there was OBS Studio malfunction with the video from of one of the in-person testing sessions, but the audio still provided useful insights so we kept it as a part of our research.
